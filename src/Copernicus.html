Copernican Astronomical Theory:

  • Who was Copernicus?
    Nicolaus Copernicus was a polish astronomer, mathematician, and philosopher. He was born on the 19th of February, 1483CE, and passed on the 24th of May, 1543CE. He was most known for his model of the solar system, which contradicted the teachings of past.
  • What did he do?
    He was most known for his theory of a heliocentric solar system, instead of a geocentric one.

  • Heliocentrism vs Geocentrism
    Heliocentrism, from greek "Helios", meaning sun, and kentron, meaning center, from this etymological breakdown, we can see that heliocentrism is the idea that the sun is the center. Geocentrism, from geo, meaning Earth / land, and centrism, meaning Earth centered. These two theories were conceived by man different philosophers, from many different cultures. For the majority of history, the geocentric model was the consensus. From the Inuit hunters in the high arctic of Canada, to the scientists in the Umayyad Islamic empire.
  • What was his legacy?
    Our current understanding of the solar system is a modified copernican heliocentric model; Copernicus also hypothesized that the rotation of the Earth was what led to a day / night cycle. In the modern day, we observed the Earth, and his ideas of the Earth spinning were confirmed. Copernicus also inspired Galileo, another famous astronomer, mathematician, and philosopher, but unlike Copernicus, Galileo was very public about his work, and was condemned by the Catholic Church at the time.
  • What did he get wrong?
    His heliocentric hypothesis stated that the planets revolved in perfect circles.
